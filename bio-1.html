Stages of Food Processing

This semester focuses on the wonders that explain how we acquire energy through consumption. Have your most curious questions answered by the Digestive System Simulation!

  • Stages of Food Processing

    Crushing Stage
    • What is being simulated by putting bananas and crackers in the zip lock?
      • Placing bananas and crackers in a ziplock bag mimics the contents of the stomach in the simulation. The bag's mixing motion replicates mechanical digestion, simulating the churning process in the stomach. Orange or lemon juice substitutes for stomach acid, promoting chemical digestion. Overall, this exercise presents a basic yet practical representation of the stomach's earliest digestive processes.
    • What is being simulated by the addition of water?
      • In the simulation, adding water to the zip-lock mimics the presence of saliva in the mouth which is swallowed into the stomach later on, which initiates the chemical breakdown of food. Saliva includes enzymes that begin the breakdown of carbohydrates. This step gives a physical depiction of the oral phase of digestion, emphasizing the significance of saliva in the early stages of digestion.
    • What does the zip-locked bag represent?
      • In the simulation, the ziplock bag represents the stomach, while the bananas and crackers represent the meal consumed. It simulates stomach movements by demonstrating both mechanical and chemical digestion. It also showed how food breaks down and formulates chyme.
    • What does crushing the food simulate?
      • Crushing the meal in the ziplock bag replicates stomach mechanical digestion. This motion is similar to churning in that it breaks down bigger food particles into tiny ones. The expanded surface area makes subsequent chemical digestion easier. Overall, it prepares the meal for optimum stomach digestion.
    • What is being simulated by the addition of orange juice?
      • The addition of orange juice to the simulation represents stomach acid, which is required for chemical digestion. The acidity mimics the low pH environment of the stomach, triggering enzymes like pepsin to break down proteins. This demonstrates how important stomach acid is in commencing the chemical breakdown of meals.
    • What do you think are the possible biomolecule components of the food inside?
      • The simulated diet, which consists of bananas and crackers, most likely includes vital biomolecules. Bananas include carbohydrates such as glucose and fructose, as well as nutritional fiber. Crackers may include carbs, lipids, and proteins, indicating a wide range of nutrients important for energy and growth.

  • Stages of Food Processing

    Transfer Stage
    • What is being simulated by the liquid flowing out of the stocking?
      • The stocking represents the tiny intestine, which absorbs glucose, amino acids, and fatty acids effectively for development and energy. This transition illustrates nutrition absorption, which supports biological activities while emphasizing the importance of the small intestine in nutrient absorption for general health.
    • What does the tray represent?
      • The tray represents the body's readiness to use nutrients. The liquid, which represents ingested nutrients from the small intestine, passes through the sock and into the circulation. This emphasizes the conclusion of digestion and the importance of nutrient absorption in overall physiological health and function.
    • What is left inside the stocking?
      • The substance left in the stocking is unabsorbed waste that will later become feces. Water absorption in the large intestine hardens the substance as the digestive process progresses. This highlights the body's efficient nutrition separation and the creation of waste products expelled as feces, signaling the end of digestion.
    • What is being simulated by pushing the partially solidified content?
      • Pushing the material from the stocking reflects the final removal of feces through the rectum and anus in the simulation. This emphasizes the importance of these structures in finishing the digestive process by eliminating waste from the body.
