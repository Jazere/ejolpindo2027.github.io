Stages of Food Processing

This semester focuses on the wonders that explain how we acquire energy through consumption. Have your most curious questions answered by the Digestive System Simulation!

  • Simulation of Absorption

    • Which paper towel absorbed the largest amount of water? What could be the possible explanation?
      • Here is our data:
        • no fold: 4 mL
        • one fold: 6 mL
        • two fold: 8 mL
        • three fold: 11 mL
        According to our data gathered, 3 folds absorbed the most water. This means that the more folds, the more it can absorb. More folds on toilet paper increase surface area, which improves its ability to absorb water by offering more contact sites for efficient liquid retention.
    • In which organ of the digestive system can you compare the paper towels? How does the structure of the organ help in its function?
      • Folds in toilet paper boost absorption efficiency, and folds in the human intestines, particularly the small intestine, play an important role. The many folds, known as villi and microvilli, greatly increase the surface area available for nutrition absorption. This structural adaptation optimizes the digestive process by increasing the number of contact sites for the absorption of important nutrients from food, thus improving the digestive system's efficiency.
