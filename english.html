Advanced Literature

This semester highlights a broad range of sentiments and cultures conveyed through poems of Western origin. Delve into a journey of love, wonder, and diversity and indulge within the collection of Sonnets from Lectio's administrators, Unspoken Wanderlust!

Unspoken Wanderlust

This collection aims to address the emotions that are not frequently voiced out due to a personal stigmatized perception on oneself, or from those around them.

The poems in this collection delve into a range of intensities: from a subtle desire to reclaim a lost memory to an eager search for romance. Every stanza captures the essence of unmet expectations, resulting in a poetic landscape that speaks to the universal issue of ‘longing’ in all of its manifestations.

Whispers of Solitude: A Love Unseen, Longing Unheard

In solitude, you weave a haunting song,
A tapestry of days now cast away,
A spectral presence in my heart does stay,
Taunting with memories that linger long.

Upon my chair, despair becomes a throng,
No sadder soul exists in dim dismay,
I sit and stare, through shadows make my way,
Gloom everywhere, where once sweet joy belonged.

Dear Lord, reverse time's cruel decree,
In solitude, my prayers, a stabilizing mangrove,
Restore what once was love, let it be.
Yet, silence reigns, a mournful symphony,
Within my chair, despair finds its alcove,
In solitude, I dwell, longing for thee.

A Ballet of Life's Lingering Lament

In the soft embrace of drunken, joyous haze
Yet heaven, now, deems me most miserable
A quest for toil, in labor's stern malaise
Life's enduring woes, in ways terrible

Within my days, why yield my prime of time
To souls indifferent to my plaintive cry
Two lovers entwined, in love's sweet paradigm
While I, in misery, unheeded sigh

Why squander life on those who scarcely care?
In existential longing, I deploy
To comprehend the suffering life may bear
Seeking labor's toil, but found despair's employ

Thus, in the haze and misery, I find
An existential quest, longing entwined

Dreams Internalized Eternally

The sky whence angels leaped are now used to mourn and weep,
Your loss and grief was now in front of you to witness,
The light broke sending me and you to madness,
You were my love, my light, mine to keep,

I refuse to let my heart be torn apart as I sleep,
